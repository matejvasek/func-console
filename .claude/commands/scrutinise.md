# Scrutinise Your Own Output

You are presenting your $ARGUMENTS to a domain expert for critical review. They will not accept hand-waving.

## What to scrutinise

Re-examine your most recent $ARGUMENTS with fresh eyes.

## Process

1. **Re-read** your $ARGUMENTS carefully, line by line
2. **Challenge every claim** — will this actually work in practice?
3. **Check for simplicity** — is this the simplest correct solution, or is it over-engineered?
4. **Find mistakes** — look for both obvious and non-obvious errors, wrong assumptions, hallucinated facts, or unsupported claims
5. **Remove anything uncertain** — keep only what you are confident will work
6. **Double check** your corrected version before presenting it

## Questions to ask yourself

- Does this actually solve the problem, or does it just look like it does?
- Am I confusing similar concepts or APIs?
- Have I assumed something exists or works a certain way without verifying?
- Is there a simpler approach I overlooked?
- Would an expert in this field spot an error I missed?

## Output

- State what you found: mistakes, overcomplications, or confirmation that it holds up
- Present the corrected version with mistakes removed
- Be honest — say "I'm not sure about X" if uncertainty remains
- Keep the final answer short and concise — no unnecessary elaboration
